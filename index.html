The Pulse of the City: Mapping Two Decades of San Francisco Crime

Between 2003 and 2026, San Francisco transitioned from a burgeoning tech hub into a complex post-pandemic metropolis. Beyond the media narrative lies a structured reality preserved in millions of police records. Our analysis reveals that urban crime is not a sequence of random events, but a system of predictable temporal rhythms and spatial concentrations.

Data Foundation & Quality Assurance

To maintain the highest standards of analytical integrity, the research team at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) has engineered a unified longitudinal dataset. This foundation was built by harmonizing two primary streams from the San Francisco Open Data Portal.

Comparative Temporal Analysis. The navy-blue series (2003–2018) captures historical volatility, while the red series (2018–2026) reflects modern structural shifts and the post-pandemic decline.

The Anatomy of Urban Incidents

When evaluating the univariate shape of San Francisco’s criminal landscape, our findings reveal a classic "Tall Peak" distribution. A disproportionate volume of total activity is concentrated within Larceny/Theft.

Categorical Frequency Distribution. Resource allocation targeting the "tallest peaks" offers the highest potential for reducing aggregate incident volumes.
